Patrice Motsepe Net Worth

South Africa's first Black billionaire, Patrice Motsepe rose from a Soweto township shop to mining magnate, building African Rainbow Minerals and a financial-services empire — and pledging half his wealth to charity.

Source of Wealth

Patrice Motsepe's fortune of around $4.3 billion in 2026 spans diversified mining, gold, digital banking, and renewable energy. He made his first fortune in mining and has steadily broadened into financial services and technology.

He founded African Rainbow Minerals (ARM) after buying and turning around marginal gold-mine shafts in the 1990s — taking on assets others viewed as played out and making them profitable.

Through African Rainbow Capital he has built exposure to financial services, including digital banking, and renewable energy, diversifying well beyond the mining cycle that made him.

Lessons Learned

What aspiring builders can take away
  • Buy what others overlook. His break came from making 'played-out' gold shafts profitable when majors walked away
  • Turn expertise into ownership. He converted legal and deal-making skill into equity, not just fees
  • Diversify past your first win. From mining he built financial services, banking, and renewable-energy interests
  • Give back deliberately. He joined the Giving Pledge, committing half his wealth to charitable causes
