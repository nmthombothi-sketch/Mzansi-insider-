How to Become a Pilot in South Africa: Licences, Costs and the Real Path

Becoming an airline pilot is expensive and demanding — but the path is clear and the top salaries are among the country's highest. Here's how it actually works.

MzansiInsider8 min readUpdated June 2026

The Licence Ladder

Becoming a pilot means climbing a ladder of licences. You start with a Private Pilot Licence (PPL), progress to a Commercial Pilot Licence (CPL) that lets you fly for money, and ultimately earn an Airline Transport Pilot Licence (ATPL), which is required to captain airline aircraft. Each stage adds flight hours, theory exams, and medical requirements.

The Cost Reality

Training is expensive. Going the private route from zero to a CPL/ATPL can cost R500,000 or more. Some pilots reduce this through airline cadet programmes, bursaries, or by instructing to build hours while earning. Be clear-eyed about the investment before you start.

Building Hours

After your CPL you rarely walk straight into an airline. Most new pilots build hours first — as flight instructors, in charter, or in regional aviation — before qualifying for airline first-officer roles. This hour-building phase is where patience and persistence pay off.

"No university degree is required to fly — but strong Maths and Physical Science make the theory exams far more manageable, and the financial commitment is real."

Medical and Theory Requirements

You'll need to pass aviation medical examinations and a series of theory exams set by the South African Civil Aviation Authority (SACAA). Maintaining a valid medical certificate is an ongoing requirement throughout your career.

What Pilots Earn

See our pilot salary guide. Pay rises steeply with hours and aircraft type — junior first officers start modestly, while wide-body international captains are among the highest earners in the country.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to become a pilot in South Africa?
Training via the private route from PPL to CPL/ATPL can cost R500,000 or more. Airline cadet programmes, bursaries, and instructing to build hours can reduce the cost.
Do you need a degree to become a pilot?
No. A pilot's licence pathway (CPL/ATPL) does not require a university degree, though strong Maths and Physical Science help with the theory exams.
How long does it take to become an airline pilot?
It varies, but several years is typical — earning your licences, then building flight hours through instructing, charter, or regional flying before qualifying for airline roles.
What licences do you need to fly commercially?
You need at least a Commercial Pilot Licence (CPL) to fly for money, and an Airline Transport Pilot Licence (ATPL) to captain airline aircraft.
