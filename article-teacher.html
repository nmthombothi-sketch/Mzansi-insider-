How to Become a Teacher in South Africa: The Complete 2026 Guide

Teaching offers stable, pensionable employment and a fully funded route in via Funza Lushaka. Here's how to qualify, register with SACE, and where the jobs are.

MzansiInsider7 min readUpdated June 2026

Two Routes Into Teaching

There are two main paths. The first is a four-year Bachelor of Education (BEd) degree, which trains you to teach from the start. The second is to complete any relevant bachelor's degree and then a one-year Postgraduate Certificate in Education (PGCE). Both lead to the same professional outcome; the PGCE suits people who already have, or want, a subject degree first.

Choose a Priority Subject

South Africa urgently needs teachers in Mathematics, Physical Science, Technology, and languages. Specialising in a scarce subject makes you far more employable and is exactly what the Funza Lushaka bursary prioritises.

Register with SACE

Every teacher must register with the South African Council for Educators (SACE) before teaching. It's the professional body that licenses educators, and registration is a legal requirement for employment in schools.

"A STEM-qualified teacher with Funza Lushaka funding has one of the most reliable graduate-to-job pipelines in the country."

Get It Funded

The Funza Lushaka Bursary fully funds students training to teach priority subjects, in exchange for teaching in a public school for the same number of years you were funded. It's one of the most reliable routes into a stable career.

What Teachers Earn

See our teacher salary guide. Public-sector pay follows a structured, transparent scale that rises with qualifications and experience, with promotion into HOD, deputy, and principal roles.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you become a teacher in South Africa?
Either complete a four-year Bachelor of Education (BEd), or complete any relevant bachelor's degree followed by a one-year Postgraduate Certificate in Education (PGCE). Then register with SACE.
What is the Funza Lushaka bursary?
Funza Lushaka is a government bursary that fully funds students training to teach priority subjects, in exchange for teaching at a public school for the same number of years they were funded.
Do teachers need to register with SACE?
Yes. All teachers must register with the South African Council for Educators (SACE) before they can teach — it is a legal requirement for school employment.
Which teaching subjects are most in demand?
Mathematics, Physical Science, Technology, and languages are the most in-demand, and are prioritised by the Funza Lushaka bursary.
