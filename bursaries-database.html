Bursary FAQs

How do I apply for a bursary in South Africa?
Most South African bursaries require an online application with your latest academic results, ID, proof of household income, and a motivation. Apply early — many close months before the academic year. Mzansi Insider lists closing dates so you never miss one.
What is the difference between a bursary and NSFAS?
NSFAS is government funding for students from households earning below a set threshold and covers tuition and living costs at public institutions. A bursary is typically funded by a company or trust, may be open to a wider range of incomes, and often includes vacation work or an employment agreement.
Do bursaries have to be paid back?
Most bursaries do not require cash repayment, but many include a 'work-back' agreement where you commit to working for the funder for a period after qualifying. Always read the contract before accepting.
Can I apply for more than one bursary?
Yes. You can and should apply to multiple bursaries to improve your chances, though you generally cannot hold two full bursaries that both cover full tuition at the same time.
