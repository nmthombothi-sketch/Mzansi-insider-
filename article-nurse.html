How to Become a Nurse in South Africa: The Complete 2026 Guide

Nursing is a scarce skill with steady demand and a clear, regulated path. Here's exactly how to qualify, register with SANC, and what to expect at each step.

MzansiInsider7 min readUpdated June 2026

Why Nursing Is a Smart Choice in 2026

South Africa faces a persistent shortage of nurses, worsened by emigration and an ageing population. That means strong, stable demand — and a profession where qualified people rarely struggle to find work. It's also a career with genuine progression, from staff nurse to specialist, manager, or educator.

The Qualification Routes

The main route is a four-year Bachelor of Nursing degree at a university or accredited nursing education institution, which qualifies you as a professional nurse. Shorter routes lead to enrolled nurse and enrolled nursing auxiliary roles, with the option to bridge upward later. Whichever you choose, the institution must be accredited by the South African Nursing Council (SANC).

Registering with SANC

You cannot practise as a nurse in South Africa without registering with the South African Nursing Council. Registration follows your qualification and is what legally allows you to work. Keep your registration active and up to date throughout your career.

"Nursing rewards those who combine clinical skill with genuine care — and it's one of the few scarce-skill careers you can enter without a maths-heavy degree."

What It Costs and How to Fund It

University nursing degrees carry standard tuition costs, but funding is widely available. The Department of Health Nursing Bursary covers tuition and a stipend in exchange for working in the public health system, and NSFAS funds qualifying students.

What Nurses Earn

See our full nurse salary guide for current figures. In short, pay rises steadily with experience and specialisation, with ICU, theatre, and management roles paying the most.

How to Start

Apply to an SANC-accredited institution, secure funding early through a nursing bursary, and choose your level (professional, enrolled, or auxiliary) based on your matric results and goals. Strong Life Sciences marks help.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to become a nurse in South Africa?
The professional nursing route is a four-year Bachelor of Nursing degree. Shorter routes to enrolled nurse or auxiliary roles take less time, with the option to bridge upward later.
Do you need to register with SANC to be a nurse?
Yes. You must register with the South African Nursing Council (SANC) after qualifying — it is what legally allows you to practise as a nurse in South Africa.
Can you study nursing for free in South Africa?
Funding is widely available. The Department of Health Nursing Bursary covers tuition and a stipend in exchange for public-sector service, and NSFAS funds qualifying students.
What subjects do you need for nursing?
Strong results in Life Sciences and related subjects help, along with meeting the admission requirements of your chosen SANC-accredited institution.
