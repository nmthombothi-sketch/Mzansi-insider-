About This Learnership

The Clicks learnership trains pharmacist assistants in dispensary support and customer care, leading to a recognised NQF Level 3 qualification. It is a practical entry point into the pharmacy and health-retail sector.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 with Mathematics and Life Sciences/Physical Science
  • Interest in pharmacy and healthcare retail
  • Unemployed youth (roughly 18–34)
  • Friendly, reliable and customer-focused

Stipend & What You Get

The Clicks learnership pays a stipend of around R4 500 a month over 12 months, plus an accredited pharmacist-assistant qualification — a recognised role with steady demand in the health-retail sector.

Application Process

Step by step
1

3

4
Attend an interview or assessment if shortlisted
5
Begin the programme if selected, combining store and dispensary work

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Qualified Pharmacist Assistant
  • Senior Pharmacist Assistant (with further study)
  • Dispensary Support Lead
  • Retail healthcare team member
  • A stepping stone toward a BPharm with further study

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the Clicks learnership pay?
It pays a stipend of around R4 500 a month over 12 months, plus an accredited NQF 3 pharmacist-assistant qualification.
What subjects do you need for the Clicks learnership?
Grade 12 with Mathematics and a science subject (Life or Physical Science) is typically expected.
Is pharmacist assistant a recognised role?
Yes. Pharmacist assistant is a recognised, regulated role with steady demand across the health-retail sector.
When does the Clicks learnership close?
Applications often run in the first half of the year. Confirm the exact date on the official Clicks careers site.
