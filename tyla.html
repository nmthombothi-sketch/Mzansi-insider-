Tyla's fortune of around $15 million in 2026 is remarkable for her age, built on the global success of 'Water' and 'Push 2 Start', two Grammy wins, and a fast-growing touring and endorsement business.

Her income spans streaming royalties, live performance fees of roughly R150 000 to R300 000 per show, a major-label and management deal, and partnerships with brands including Nike, Maybelline, and Pandora.

She has built a distinctive 'Afro-pop meets amapiano' sound and a strong visual brand, performing the anthem at the 2026 World Cup opening — turning South African identity into global appeal.
