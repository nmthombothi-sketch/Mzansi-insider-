About This Bursary

The Ikusasa Student Financial Aid Programme (ISFAP) supports 'missing middle' students who do not qualify for NSFAS, funding studies in scarce-skill and priority fields. It combines financial cover with mentorship, tutoring, and wellness support to improve success rates.

Requirements

  • South African citizen
  • Household income above the NSFAS threshold but unable to afford fees (the 'missing middle')
  • Accepted into a supported scarce-skill qualification
  • Strong academic results
  • Demonstrated financial need

What It Covers

  • Tuition and registration support
  • Accommodation and meals
  • Learning materials and allowances
  • Mentorship, tutoring and wellness support
  • A focus on academic success, not just funding

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the ISFAP bursary for?
The 'missing middle' — students whose household income is above the NSFAS threshold but who still cannot afford university fees.
What fields does ISFAP fund?
Scarce-skill and priority qualifications, combined with mentorship, tutoring, and wellness support to improve success.
How is ISFAP different from NSFAS?
NSFAS funds lower-income students below a set threshold, while ISFAP targets the 'missing middle' who earn too much for NSFAS but too little to self-fund.
When does ISFAP close?
Applications typically align with the start-of-year cycle. Confirm the exact date on the official ISFAP site.
