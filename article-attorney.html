Becoming an Attorney in South Africa: LLB, Articles, Admission and the Big Law Pay Scale

The legal profession's gatekeeping is severe and the financial rewards are polarised. Here is an honest account of what it takes and what you will earn at each stage.

MzansiInsider10 min readUpdated June 2026

The LLB

The four-year Bachelor of Laws is the foundation. A strong academic record matters enormously — the top commercial firms recruit almost exclusively from the upper tier of graduates at UCT, Wits, Stellenbosch, UP, and UKZN.

Articles of Clerkship

A two-year training contract at a law firm (one year if you complete the practical legal training course). Competition for articles at the big commercial firms is fierce and usually begins in your penultimate year of study.

Admission

Pass the board exams set by the Legal Practice Council, complete your articles, and apply to the High Court to be admitted as an attorney. Only then can you practise independently.

"Big Law in Sandton pays exceptionally well, but the hours are relentless. The profession rewards stamina as much as intellect."

Attorney vs Advocate

Attorneys deal directly with clients and handle transactional and litigation work; advocates are specialist courtroom litigators briefed by attorneys, operating as referral counsel at the Bar. The training paths diverge after the LLB.

The Pay Scale

  • Candidate Attorney (during articles): R12,000 – R30,000/month
  • Newly admitted (commercial firm): R420,000 – R650,000/year
  • Senior Associate: R900,000 – R1.4m/year
  • Partner / Director (Big Law): R2.5m+/year

Earnings at smaller firms and in public-interest law are considerably lower, so the profession's financial rewards are highly polarised by where you land.
