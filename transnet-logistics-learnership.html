About This Learnership

Transnet's supply chain and logistics learnership gives learners exposure across South Africa's freight network — ports, rail, and pipelines. The TETA-accredited programme builds practical logistics and supply-chain skills that are in steady demand thanks to e-commerce and trade growth, and it offers a credible pathway into government and private-sector logistics roles.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 / Matric
  • Aged 18–35 in most intakes
  • Interest in logistics, supply chain and operations
  • Willingness to rotate across operational sites

Stipend & What You Get

Learners receive a monthly stipend of around R4 700 over the 12-month programme while completing an accredited NQF 4 logistics qualification and gaining operational experience across Transnet's businesses. The stipend covers basic living costs during the learnership.

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Logistics coordinator and supply-chain roles
  • Operations and warehousing positions
  • Procurement and freight roles in public and private sectors
  • A foundation for further supply-chain qualifications

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the Transnet logistics learnership pay?
It pays a monthly stipend of around R4 700 over the 12-month programme while you complete an accredited NQF 4 logistics qualification.
What qualification does it lead to?
An accredited NQF Level 4 qualification in logistics / supply chain, recognised across the transport and logistics sector.
Who can apply for the Transnet learnership?
South African citizens with a Grade 12 / Matric and an interest in logistics and operations, willing to rotate across operational sites.
What careers can it lead to?
Logistics coordination, operations, warehousing, and procurement roles across both the public and private sectors, with room to grow through further qualifications.
