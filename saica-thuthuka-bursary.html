About This Bursary

The Thuthuka Bursary Fund is run by SAICA to transform the chartered accountancy profession in South Africa. It funds students at accredited universities through a BCom/BAcc degree and provides extensive academic and personal support designed to carry students all the way to the CA(SA) qualification. It is one of the most generous and supportive bursaries in the commerce space.

Requirements

  • South African citizen of African or Coloured descent (transformation mandate)
  • Strong matric results, particularly in Mathematics (not Maths Literacy)
  • Studying towards a SAICA-accredited BCom/BAcc degree at a partner university
  • Demonstrated financial need

What It Covers

  • Full tuition fees
  • Accommodation and meals
  • Textbooks and a laptop allowance
  • Intensive academic support and tutoring
  • Mentorship toward the CA(SA) qualification

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for the Thuthuka bursary?
South African citizens of African or Coloured descent with strong Mathematics results who are studying a SAICA-accredited BCom or BAcc degree and have financial need. The fund has a transformation mandate.
Does Thuthuka require Pure Maths?
Yes — a strong mark in Pure Mathematics (not Mathematical Literacy) is essential, as accounting and the CA(SA) route are mathematically demanding.
What does the Thuthuka bursary cover?
Full tuition, accommodation, meals, books and a laptop allowance, plus intensive academic support and mentorship toward becoming a CA(SA).
When does the Thuthuka bursary close?
Applications for 2026 close on 31 May 2026. Confirm on the official Thuthuka Bursary site, as dates change yearly.
