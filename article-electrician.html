How to Become an Electrician in South Africa: Trade Test and Beyond

Electrical work is a strong-earning trade you can enter without a university degree — and specialists can out-earn many graduates. Here's the practical path.

MzansiInsider7 min readUpdated June 2026

Why the Trades Make Sense

Not everyone needs a degree to earn well. Electrical work is in steady demand thanks to ongoing infrastructure, construction, and energy projects, and qualified specialists can earn very comfortably. It's hands-on, practical, and far cheaper to train for than university.

The Apprenticeship Route

The most common path is an apprenticeship: working under a qualified electrician while learning the trade, often combined with theory at a TVET college. This earns you the experience and knowledge you'll need to attempt the trade test.

The Trade Test

Becoming a qualified electrician means passing a trade test, which certifies your competence. This is the key milestone that separates apprentices from qualified, well-paid tradespeople.

"A trade-tested electrician with a wireman's licence and a specialisation can earn more than many degree-holding professionals — without the student debt."

The Wireman's Licence

To certify electrical installations as compliant, you'll want a wireman's licence. This raises your earning power significantly, as it lets you sign off work that others can't.

Specialise to Earn More

The highest earners specialise — in industrial, high-voltage, or solar and renewable installations — or run their own businesses. Mining and industrial sites also pay a premium. See our electrician salary guide for the ranges.

Get Started

Look for an apprenticeship with a registered employer or enrol at a TVET college. A construction or technical learnership can also be a useful entry point.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you become a qualified electrician in South Africa?
Through an apprenticeship with a registered employer or a TVET college pathway, followed by a trade test that certifies your competence as a qualified electrician.
Can you earn good money as an electrician without a degree?
Yes. Trade-tested electricians who specialise (industrial, high-voltage, solar) or hold a wireman's licence can earn R40,000 to R60,000 or more per month at senior level.
What is a wireman's licence?
A wireman's licence allows you to certify electrical installations as compliant. It raises your earning power because you can sign off work that uncertified electricians cannot.
How long does it take to become an electrician?
It varies, but several years of apprenticeship and on-the-job experience are typical before you're ready to pass the trade test and qualify.
