How to Build a LinkedIn Profile That Gets You Hired

Recruiters search LinkedIn constantly. A well-optimised profile turns you from invisible to in-demand. Here's how to build one.

MzansiInsider6 min readUpdated June 2026

Why LinkedIn Matters

Many South African recruiters source candidates directly on LinkedIn, often before a job is even advertised. A strong profile means opportunities come to you.

Nail Your Headline

Your headline is the most-read part of your profile. Don't just list your job title — describe what you do and the value you bring, with keywords recruiters search for.

Write a Real "About" Section

Write your About in the first person, telling a short, genuine story: what you do, what you're good at, and what you're looking for. Make it human, not a list of buzzwords.

"Recruiters search by keyword — if your profile doesn't contain the terms they're searching for, you simply don't appear, no matter how good you are."

Use Keywords Strategically

Sprinkle the skills and terms relevant to your field naturally throughout your headline, About, and experience. This is what makes you findable in recruiter searches.

Show, Don't Just Tell

Quantify achievements in your experience section — numbers, results, impact. Add skills, request recommendations, and keep your photo professional and friendly.

Network with Intent

Connect with people in your industry, engage with their posts thoughtfully, and share the occasional useful insight. Visibility compounds over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I optimise my LinkedIn profile?
Write a value-focused headline with keywords, a genuine first-person About section, quantified achievements, relevant skills, a professional photo, and network with intent.
Why do keywords matter on LinkedIn?
Recruiters search LinkedIn by keyword. If your profile doesn't contain the terms they search for, you won't appear in their results, regardless of your ability.
What makes a good LinkedIn headline?
One that describes what you do and the value you bring, using keywords recruiters search for — not just your job title.
Does networking on LinkedIn help with jobs?
Yes. Connecting with people in your industry, engaging thoughtfully, and sharing useful insights builds visibility, which compounds into opportunities over time.
