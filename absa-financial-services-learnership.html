About This Learnership

Absa's financial services learnership develops learners in banking and financial-services operations toward an accredited NQF 5 qualification. It blends classroom learning with workplace experience, and high performers are well placed for permanent consultant and banking roles. For many matriculants it's a structured, paid entry into the financial-services industry.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 / Matric with Mathematics or Mathematical Literacy
  • Aged 18–28 in most intakes
  • Strong numeracy and communication skills
  • No similar learnership already completed

Stipend & What You Get

Learners receive a monthly stipend of around R4 700 over the 12-month programme while working toward an accredited NQF 5 financial-services qualification. The stipend supports living costs during training rather than serving as a full salary.

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Permanent banking consultant and sales roles
  • Customer-service and operations positions
  • A foundation for further financial-services qualifications
  • Transferable experience across the banking sector

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the ABSA learnership pay?
It pays a monthly stipend of around R4 700 over the 12-month programme while you work toward an accredited NQF 5 financial-services qualification.
What are the requirements for the ABSA learnership?
A Grade 12 / Matric with Mathematics or Mathematical Literacy, strong numeracy and communication skills, and being within the typical age range for the intake.
Can the ABSA learnership lead to permanent work?
Yes. High performers are well placed for permanent consultant, sales, and operations roles at Absa after completing the programme.
What qualification do you earn?
An accredited NQF Level 5 financial-services qualification, recognised across the banking sector.
