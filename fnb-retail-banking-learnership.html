About This Learnership

FNB's retail banking learnership combines classroom training with on-the-job experience in retail and business banking. Learners earn a stipend while working toward an accredited FETC in Banking (NQF 5). High performers are frequently considered for permanent positions as tellers, consultants, or in business banking, making it one of the stronger entry routes into the financial-services sector for matriculants.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 / Matric with Mathematics or Mathematical Literacy
  • Aged 18–25 in most intakes
  • No previous learnership of the same kind completed
  • Strong communication skills and a customer-service orientation

Stipend & What You Get

Learners receive a monthly stipend of around R5 000 for the 12-month programme, along with structured training toward an accredited banking qualification. The stipend is intended to cover transport and basic costs while you gain workplace experience — it is a training allowance rather than a full salary.

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Permanent teller or banking consultant roles at FNB
  • Business banking and sales consultant positions
  • A foundation for further banking qualifications
  • Transferable financial-services experience across the big-four banks

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the FNB learnership pay?
The FNB retail banking learnership pays a monthly stipend of around R5 000 for the 12-month programme. This is a training allowance to cover basic costs, not a full salary.
What qualification do you get?
Learners work toward an accredited Further Education and Training Certificate (FETC) in Banking at NQF Level 5, recognised across the financial-services sector.
Do you need Matric for the FNB learnership?
Yes. A Grade 12 / Matric certificate is required, typically with Mathematics or Mathematical Literacy, along with strong communication skills.
Can the learnership lead to a permanent job?
Yes. Strong performers are frequently considered for permanent teller, consultant, or business-banking roles at FNB after completing the programme.
