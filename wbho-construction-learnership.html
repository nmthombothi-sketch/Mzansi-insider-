About This Learnership

WBHO's civil works learnership trains learners in construction and civil engineering support at NQF Level 4, accredited by the CETA. With ongoing infrastructure investment, construction offers steady demand for skilled site personnel. Learners gain practical experience on real projects alongside a stipend, building toward supervisory and technical roles on site.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 / Matric, ideally with Mathematics and Physical Science
  • Aged 18–30 in most intakes
  • Physically able to work on construction sites
  • Interest in civil engineering and the built environment

Stipend & What You Get

Learners receive a monthly stipend of around R4 000 over the 18-month programme while completing an accredited NQF 4 construction qualification and gaining hands-on site experience. The stipend supports living costs during training.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the WBHO construction learnership pay?
It pays a monthly stipend of around R4 000 over the 18-month programme while you complete an accredited NQF 4 construction qualification.
What does the WBHO learnership involve?
Hands-on training in civil works and construction support on real projects, accredited by the CETA, alongside classroom learning.
Who can apply for the WBHO learnership?
South African citizens with a Grade 12 / Matric (ideally with Maths and Physical Science) who are physically able to work on construction sites and interested in the built environment.
What careers can it lead to?
Construction supervisor, site foreman, and civil works technician roles, and a pathway toward further engineering qualifications.
