Elon Musk Net Worth

Source of Wealth

Elon Musk's fortune of around $1.3 trillion in 2026 made him the world's first US-dollar trillionaire, following the SpaceX IPO in June 2026. He is South African-born but resident in the United States.

His wealth is concentrated in equity stakes across Tesla (electric vehicles and energy), SpaceX (rockets and Starlink satellite internet), and xAI (artificial intelligence), plus interests including X and Neuralink.

Musk left South Africa as a teenager, studied in Canada and the US, and built his fortune entirely abroad — so while he tops any list of South African-born billionaires, he is not a South African resident.

Companies & Major Holdings

  • Tesla — Electric vehicles and energy; the foundation of his public wealth
  • SpaceX — Rockets and the Starlink satellite-internet network; IPO'd in 2026
  • xAI — His artificial-intelligence company
  • X / Neuralink — Social media and neurotechnology interests

Lessons Learned

What aspiring builders can take away
  • Tackle enormous problems. Energy, space, and AI are vast markets with huge upside
  • Concentrate your bets. His wealth comes from large equity stakes, not diversification
  • Reinvest relentlessly. Early exits funded ever-bigger, riskier ventures
  • Tolerate extreme risk. He repeatedly staked fortunes on companies others called impossible

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Elon Musk's net worth in 2026?
Elon Musk's net worth is around $1.3 trillion in 2026, making him the world's first US-dollar trillionaire following the SpaceX IPO in June 2026.
Is Elon Musk South African?
Yes, he was born in Pretoria in 1971, but he left as a teenager and is now resident in the United States, where he built his entire fortune.
How did Elon Musk make his money?
He made early money from Zip2 and PayPal, then built enormous equity wealth through Tesla, SpaceX, and more recently xAI.
Is Elon Musk the richest South African?
