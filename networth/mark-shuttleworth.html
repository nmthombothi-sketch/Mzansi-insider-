Source of Wealth

Mark Shuttleworth's fortune, estimated around $500 million in 2026, originated with the 1999 sale of Thawte — the digital-certificate company he built — to VeriSign for roughly $575 million, an extraordinary outcome for a young founder.

He reinvested that capital into Canonical, the company behind the hugely influential Ubuntu Linux operating system, and into venture investing through his HBD vehicle and the Shuttleworth Foundation.

In 2002 he became the first African in space, self-funding a trip to the International Space Station — a vivid example of converting a tech windfall into bold ventures.
