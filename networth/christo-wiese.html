Christo Wiese Net Worth

Source of Wealth

Christo Wiese's fortune of around $1.7 billion in 2026 rests on decades of retail empire-building, principally Shoprite (Africa's largest grocery retailer) and Pepkor (the discount clothing and goods group behind PEP and Ackermans).

His wealth took a severe hit in the 2017 Steinhoff accounting scandal, which wiped out billions of his net worth almost overnight — a reminder that even seasoned investors face catastrophic, concentrated risk.

He has spent the years since rebuilding and refocusing on the retail and investment assets he understands best, demonstrating resilience after one of the largest personal wealth reversals in SA history.

Companies & Major Holdings

  • Shoprite — Africa's largest supermarket retailer, which he built into a continental force
  • Pepkor — Discount retail group — PEP, Ackermans and others
  • Brait — Investment holding company
  • Invicta / others — Industrial and investment interests

Lessons Learned

What aspiring builders can take away
  • Master one sector deeply. His durable wealth came from retail, an industry he understood better than anyone
  • Concentration cuts both ways. Steinhoff showed how a single bad holding can erase decades of gains
  • Resilience matters. He rebuilt methodically after a historic reversal rather than retreating
  • Scale across borders. He grew SA retail brands across the continent, multiplying the market
