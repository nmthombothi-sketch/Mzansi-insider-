About This Bursary

The National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) funds qualifying South African students at all 26 public universities and TVET colleges. It is means-tested: funding is aimed at students from households earning below the annual threshold. Unlike a loan, NSFAS funding does not have to be repaid as long as you remain academically eligible and meet the scheme's conditions, making it the backbone of access to higher education in South Africa.

Requirements

  • South African citizen
  • Combined household income below the NSFAS threshold (typically R350 000 per year; higher for students with disabilities)
  • Accepted or applying to a public university or TVET college
  • Meeting academic progression requirements to keep funding

What It Covers

  • Full tuition fees
  • Accommodation or a transport allowance
  • Learning materials allowance
  • Monthly living allowance
  • No repayment while you meet the conditions

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for NSFAS in 2026?
South African citizens accepted at a public university or TVET college whose combined household income is below the NSFAS threshold (typically around R350 000 per year, and higher for students with disabilities).
Does NSFAS have to be repaid?
NSFAS funding does not have to be repaid as long as you remain academically eligible and meet the scheme's conditions. It functions as a bursary rather than a loan for qualifying students.
What does NSFAS cover?
NSFAS covers tuition, accommodation or a transport allowance, a learning materials allowance, and a monthly living allowance.
When do NSFAS applications close?
Applications for the 2026 academic year typically close towards the end of the preceding year — confirm the exact date on the official myNSFAS portal, as it changes annually.
