About This Learnership

Shoprite's retail learnership trains learners in wholesale and retail operations at NQF Level 3. Crucially, many intakes are open to applicants without a full Matric, making it one of the most accessible entry points into the workforce. Learners earn a stipend while gaining an accredited qualification and real store experience, with strong prospects of permanent retail employment afterwards.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Matric not always required (Grade 10/11 may be accepted)
  • Aged 18–34 in most intakes
  • Unemployed and not studying full-time
  • Willingness to work retail hours, including weekends

Stipend & What You Get

Learners receive a monthly stipend of around R3 100 over the 12-month programme while completing an accredited NQF 3 wholesale and retail qualification. The stipend supports basic living costs during training; many learners are offered permanent roles afterwards.

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Permanent cashier, merchandiser and supervisor roles
  • Store management trainee pathways
  • Transferable retail and customer-service experience
  • A foundation for further retail qualifications

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you need Matric for the Shoprite learnership?
Not always. Many Shoprite retail learnership intakes accept applicants without a full Matric (Grade 10 or 11 may be accepted), making it one of the most accessible programmes available.
How much does the Shoprite learnership pay?
It pays a monthly stipend of around R3 100 over the 12-month programme while you complete an accredited NQF 3 retail qualification.
Can the Shoprite learnership lead to a job?
Yes. Many learners are offered permanent roles as cashiers, merchandisers, or supervisors, and some progress onto store-management trainee pathways.
Who can apply for the Shoprite learnership?
Unemployed South African citizens, typically aged 18–34, who are not studying full-time and are willing to work retail hours including weekends.
