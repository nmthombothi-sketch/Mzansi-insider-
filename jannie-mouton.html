Jannie Mouton Net Worth

Source of Wealth

Jannie Mouton's fortune of roughly $1.3 billion in 2026 stems from PSG Group, the investment holding company he founded in 1995 after being ousted from the stockbroking firm he had co-founded.

His best-known bet was backing Capitec early; PSG's stakes across Capitec, Curro (private education), Zeder (agriculture), and PSG Konsult (financial services) compounded into one of post-apartheid South Africa's great investment stories.

He holds roughly 5% of Capitec through his family trust, and that exposure alone underpins much of his billionaire status as the bank's shares have climbed.

Companies & Major Holdings

  • PSG Group — Investment holding company he founded in 1995 (now an unlisted incubator)
  • Capitec — ~5% via the family trust — his signature investment
  • Curro Holdings — Private education group backed by PSG
  • PSG Konsult / Zeder — Financial services and agribusiness interests

Lessons Learned

What aspiring builders can take away
  • Turn setbacks into fuel. Being fired at 50 became the catalyst for his greatest success
  • Invest for the long term. His 'Boere Buffett' approach prized patience and strong management teams
  • Back great businesses early. The early Capitec bet shows the power of conviction in a young company
  • It's never too late. He started building his fortune at an age when many wind down

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Jannie Mouton's net worth in 2026?

How did Jannie Mouton make his money?

Why is Jannie Mouton called the 'Boere Buffett'?
