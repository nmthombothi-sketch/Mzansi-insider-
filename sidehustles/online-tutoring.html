Private & Online Tutoring in South Africa

With matric results a perennial national concern, tutors in Maths, Science, English and Accounting can keep a full diary — and teaching English online opens foreign-currency income.

Overview

Tutoring splits into two routes: local in-person or online tutoring of South African students (especially in scarce subjects like Maths and Physical Science), and teaching English to international students through global platforms.

It needs no startup capital — just subject knowledge, patience, and a reliable internet connection for online work.

Realistic Income Breakdown

Local tutors charge R150–R400 per hour, with qualified teachers at the higher end. A part-time diary of 10–15 hours a week realistically brings R2 000–R8 000 a month. Teaching English online via platforms like Cambly or Preply pays in dollars or euros, and committed tutors can reach R20 000 a month.

How to Start

Step by step
1

2

4

5

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can you earn tutoring in South Africa?
Local tutors charge R150–R400 per hour; a part-time diary brings R2 000–R8 000 a month. Teaching English online for foreign currency can push committed tutors to around R20 000 a month.
Do you need to be a teacher to tutor?
No. Strong subject knowledge and the ability to explain clearly are what matter most, though qualified teachers can charge premium rates.
Which subjects are most in demand for tutoring?
Mathematics, Physical Science, Accounting, and English are consistently in demand, given their importance for matric results and university entrance.
Can South Africans teach English online for foreign currency?
Yes. Platforms like Cambly and Preply let South Africans teach English to students abroad, paid in dollars or euros, which the rand exchange rate amplifies.

Don't Forget SARS

Side-hustle income is taxable. Keep records from day one, separate business from personal money, and set aside a portion for tax. Once you earn beyond the threshold you may need to register as a provisional taxpayer.
