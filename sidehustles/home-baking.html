Home Baking & Meal Prep in South Africa

Overview

Home baking and meal prep covers cakes and treats, office lunch deliveries, and weekly meal-prep packs. It's one of the most accessible food hustles, started from your existing kitchen with modest outlay on ingredients and packaging.

The biggest risk isn't demand — it's pricing. Many sellers celebrate turnover while forgetting to cost ingredients, gas, and packaging properly, so margins quietly disappear.

Realistic Income Breakdown

Realistic earnings run R2 000–R12 000 a month depending on volume and pricing discipline. The key is to price each item only after costing ingredients, packaging, and gas, then build repeat orders through WhatsApp status and local Facebook groups. Office lunch deliveries near you can add steady weekday income.

How to Start

Step by step
1
Pick one or two signature items you can make consistently well
2
Cost every input — ingredients, packaging, gas — then price for real profit
3
Photograph your products well and post on WhatsApp status and local groups
4
Offer office lunch deliveries in your area for repeat weekday orders
5
Track profit per order and reinvest into better equipment and ingredients

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can you earn from home baking in South Africa?
Realistic earnings run R2 000–R12 000 a month depending on volume and pricing. The biggest factor is pricing discipline — costing ingredients, packaging, and gas properly.
How much does it cost to start home baking?
Startup is modest — around R500–R1 500 for initial ingredients and packaging, since you use your existing kitchen and equipment.
How do you find customers for home baking?
Post good photos on WhatsApp status and local Facebook groups, offer office lunch deliveries in your area, and build repeat orders through reliable quality.
What's the biggest mistake home bakers make?
Mispricing. Many focus on turnover and forget to cost ingredients, packaging, and gas, so real profit disappears. Always price after costing every input.

Don't Forget SARS

Side-hustle income is taxable. Keep records from day one, separate business from personal money, and set aside a portion for tax. Once you earn beyond the threshold you may need to register as a provisional taxpayer.
