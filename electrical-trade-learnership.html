About This Learnership

The Electrical learnership, through the merSETA, trains learners in electrical systems and industrial maintenance, leading toward an electrician trade test. Qualified, trade-tested electricians are in steady demand and earn well.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 with Mathematics and Physical Science (preferred)
  • Interest in electrical and industrial work
  • Unemployed youth (roughly 18–35)
  • Physically able to do hands-on technical work

Stipend & What You Get

The electrical learnership pays a stipend of around R5 500 a month, plus a merSETA qualification and a path toward an electrician trade test and wireman's licence — which unlock strong earnings in the trade.

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Qualified Electrician (with trade test)
  • Industrial Maintenance Technician
  • Wireman (with a wireman's licence)
  • High-voltage or solar specialist (with experience)
  • A self-employed electrical contractor

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the electrical learnership pay?
It pays a stipend of around R5 500 a month, plus a merSETA qualification and a path toward an electrician trade test.
Is electrical work a good trade?
Yes. Qualified, trade-tested electricians are in steady demand and are among the best-earning trades, especially with a wireman's licence or specialisation.
What subjects do you need?
Grade 12 with Mathematics and Physical Science is preferred for the electrical streams.
Where do you find electrical learnerships?
They are advertised by companies funded by the merSETA, on company careers pages and SETA portals.
