About This Bursary

The Sasol Bursary Programme funds students in chemical, mechanical, electrical, mining and related engineering fields, as well as the sciences that feed Sasol's operations. Beyond covering the full cost of study, it offers structured vacation work and strong prospects of employment after graduation, making it one of the most sought-after corporate bursaries in the country.

Requirements

  • South African citizen
  • Strong matric or current university results, especially in Mathematics and Physical Science
  • Studying or intending to study an eligible engineering, science or technology degree
  • Financial need may be considered alongside academic merit

What It Covers

  • Full tuition fees
  • Accommodation and meals
  • Prescribed textbooks and a laptop
  • Monthly living allowance
  • Vacation work and mentorship
  • Potential employment with Sasol after graduating

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Sasol bursary cover?
The Sasol Bursary Programme typically covers full tuition, accommodation, meals, prescribed books, a laptop and a monthly allowance, plus vacation work and mentorship.
Who qualifies for the Sasol bursary?
South African citizens with strong results in Mathematics and Physical Science who are studying, or intending to study, an eligible engineering, science or technology degree.
When does the Sasol bursary close?
Applications for the 2026 intake close on 30 June 2026. Dates can change year to year, so always confirm on the official Sasol bursaries site.
Do I have to work for Sasol afterwards?
The bursary offers strong prospects of employment with Sasol and may include a work-back expectation. Check the current terms on the official application.
