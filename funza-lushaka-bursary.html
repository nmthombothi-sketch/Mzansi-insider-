About This Bursary

The Funza Lushaka Bursary Programme funds initial teacher education for students who commit to teaching priority subjects — particularly Mathematics, Science, technology and African languages — at a public school after qualifying. It is a multi-year, full-cost bursary aimed directly at South Africa's chronic shortage of qualified STEM and language teachers.

Requirements

  • South African citizen with a valid ID
  • Strong academic results, especially in priority subjects
  • Studying a BEd or a PGCE in a priority specialisation
  • Willingness to teach at a public school for the number of years funded

What It Covers

  • Full tuition fees
  • Accommodation and meals
  • Books and learning materials
  • A living allowance
  • A guaranteed teaching placement on qualifying

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the work-back commitment for Funza Lushaka?
Recipients must teach at a public school for the same number of years they received funding. The provincial education department places you in a school after you qualify.
Which subjects does Funza Lushaka prioritise?
Priority areas typically include Mathematics, Physical Science, technology, and African languages, reflecting South Africa's biggest teaching shortages.
What does Funza Lushaka cover?
Full tuition, accommodation, meals, books, and a living allowance, plus a guaranteed teaching placement once you qualify.
When does Funza Lushaka close?
Applications for the 2026 academic year typically close in January 2026. Confirm the exact date on the official Funza Lushaka portal.
