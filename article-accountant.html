How to Become an Accountant in South Africa: CA(SA) and Beyond

Accounting is one of the most reliable routes to strong, stable earnings — and the CA(SA) is its gold standard. Here are all the routes in.

MzansiInsider7 min readUpdated June 2026

More Than One Designation

"Accountant" covers several professional routes. The most prestigious is the CA(SA) via SAICA, but there are also routes through SAIPA (professional accountant), CIMA (management accounting), and ACCA. Each suits different goals.

The CA(SA) Route

This means a SAICA-accredited BCom Accounting degree, then a postgraduate qualification (CTA/Honours), a three-year training contract (articles) usually at an audit firm, and the professional exams. It's demanding but opens the most doors.

Articles

Your training contract is where theory meets practice. Pay is modest during articles but rises sharply once you qualify — see our accountant salary guide and our full CA(SA) guide.

"The CA(SA) is one of the most versatile qualifications in the country — it opens doors well beyond accounting, into finance leadership and the C-suite."

Get It Funded

The SAICA Thuthuka Bursary fully funds African and Coloured students toward the CA(SA), with strong academic support.

Where It Leads

Accounting leads to roles from financial manager to CFO. See our financial manager guide for the executive ceiling.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you become a CA(SA)?
A SAICA-accredited BCom Accounting degree, then a postgraduate CTA/Honours, a three-year training contract (articles), and the professional exams set by SAICA.
Are there other accounting designations?
Yes. Besides the CA(SA) via SAICA, there are routes through SAIPA (professional accountant), CIMA (management accounting), and ACCA, each suiting different goals.
How much do accountants earn in South Africa?
Pay rises sharply after qualifying. See our accountant salary guide for current ranges, with CA(SA)s and financial managers among the better-paid professionals.
Can you get a bursary to study accounting?
Yes. The SAICA Thuthuka Bursary fully funds qualifying students toward the CA(SA), with academic and personal support.
