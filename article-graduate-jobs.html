How to Find a Graduate Job in South Africa

With youth unemployment among the world's highest, landing a first job takes strategy. Here's how to stand out and where to look.

MzansiInsider7 min readUpdated June 2026

The Hard Truth

South Africa's youth unemployment is among the highest in the world, and many graduates struggle to find that crucial first role. The good news: employers report real skills shortages in key fields, so the right preparation makes a big difference.

Target Graduate Programmes

Many large employers — banks, retailers, mining houses, the big audit firms — run structured graduate programmes. These are designed to take graduates with little experience and train them, making them ideal first roles. Apply early; intakes fill fast.

Build Experience Before You Graduate

Internships, vacation work, learnerships, and even volunteering set you apart. Practical experience is what employers value most, and a learnership can be a direct route into a company.

"The graduates who get hired aren't always the ones with the best marks — they're the ones who can show practical experience and the ability to add value."

Choose an In-Demand Field

Your qualification matters. Engineering, IT, healthcare, accounting, and the trades have far better prospects — see our most employable qualifications report.

Get the Basics Right

A strong CV, an optimised LinkedIn profile, and solid interview preparation are the fundamentals. Don't neglect them.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find a graduate job in South Africa?
Target structured graduate programmes at large employers, build experience through internships and learnerships, choose an in-demand field, and get your CV, LinkedIn, and interview prep right.
What are graduate programmes?
Structured training programmes run by large employers — banks, retailers, mining houses, audit firms — designed to take graduates with little experience and develop them into permanent staff.
How can graduates stand out despite high unemployment?
By gaining practical experience through internships, vacation work, and learnerships, choosing an in-demand qualification, and demonstrating the ability to add value.
Which fields have the best graduate job prospects?
Engineering, IT, healthcare, accounting, and the skilled trades have the strongest prospects, as they map onto South Africa's scarce-skills needs.
