About This Learnership

MTN's ICT learnership trains learners in information and communications technology — IT support, networks, and digital skills — accredited through the MICT SETA. It builds capabilities that are in high demand across South Africa's digital economy.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 with Mathematics (or a relevant qualification)
  • Interest in IT and technology
  • Unemployed youth (roughly 18–35)
  • Basic computer literacy and problem-solving skills

Stipend & What You Get

MTN's ICT learnership pays a stipend of around R6 000 a month over 12 months, plus a MICT SETA-accredited IT qualification — valuable given the scarcity of digital skills.

Application Process

Step by step
1
Apply via the MTN careers portal when the learnership opens
2
Complete the online application
3
Upload matric, certified ID and CV
4
Complete aptitude and technical assessments
5
Attend an interview if shortlisted, then begin if selected

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • IT Support Technician / Help Desk
  • Network Support Technician
  • Systems Administrator (with experience)
  • Junior cybersecurity or cloud roles, with further study
  • A pathway toward software or data careers

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the MTN ICT learnership pay?
It pays a stipend of around R6 000 a month over 12 months, plus a MICT SETA-accredited IT qualification.
Do you need Maths for the MTN learnership?
Grade 12 with Mathematics, or a relevant qualification, is typically required for the technical ICT streams.

Yes. It is accredited through the MICT SETA, so the qualification is nationally recognised on the NQF.
