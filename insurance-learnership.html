About This Learnership

The Long-Term Insurance learnership, through INSETA, trains learners in insurance products, claims processing, risk assessment, and customer service, leading to an NQF Level 4 qualification and a route into the insurance industry.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 / Matric (Maths or Maths Literacy)
  • Interest in insurance, finance or customer service
  • Unemployed youth (roughly 18–35)
  • Good communication and numeracy

Stipend & What You Get

The insurance learnership pays a stipend of around R5 000 a month over 12 months, plus an INSETA NQF 4 qualification. Insurance is a stable, sizeable employer offering clear progression for those who qualify.

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Insurance Claims Administrator
  • Customer Service Consultant
  • Underwriting Assistant
  • Broker Support / Sales (with experience)
  • A pathway toward financial advisory roles

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the insurance learnership pay?
It pays a stipend of around R5 000 a month over 12 months, plus an INSETA NQF 4 insurance qualification.
What does the insurance learnership cover?
Insurance products, claims processing, risk assessment, and customer service within the insurance industry.
Who can apply for the insurance learnership?
Unemployed South African youth with a matric, good communication and numeracy, and an interest in insurance or finance.
Where do you find insurance learnerships?
They are advertised by companies funded by INSETA, on company careers pages and SETA portals.
