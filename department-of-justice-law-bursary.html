About This Bursary

The Department of Justice and Constitutional Development Law Bursary funds students studying towards an LLB at accredited South African universities. It covers the cost of study and, in return, recipients commit to a work-back agreement within the justice system, helping build capacity in courts, prosecution, and legal services.

Requirements

  • South African citizen
  • Registered or accepted for an LLB at an accredited university
  • Strong academic results
  • Demonstrated financial need
  • Commitment to working in the justice system after qualifying

What It Covers

  • Full tuition and registration fees
  • Accommodation and meals
  • Books and a living allowance
  • Practical exposure within the justice system
  • A path to employment in the public legal sector

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Department of Justice law bursary cover?
It typically covers full tuition, accommodation, books, and a living allowance for LLB students, plus practical exposure within the justice system.
Is there a work-back obligation?
Yes. In return for funding, recipients commit to serving in the justice system for a set period after qualifying.
Who can apply for the law bursary?
South African citizens registered or accepted for an LLB at an accredited university, with strong academic results and demonstrated financial need.
When does the Department of Justice law bursary close?
Applications usually close in the second half of the year. Confirm the exact date on the official Department of Justice site.
