About This Learnership

The Basic Traffic Officer learnership, run by provincial departments, trains unemployed youth toward becoming qualified traffic officers, combining classroom theory, physical training, and practical road-traffic experience.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 / Matric
  • Valid driver's licence (Code B, often required)
  • Aged roughly 18–35
  • Physically fit, with a clear criminal record
  • Willing to undergo physical and disciplinary training

Stipend & What You Get

The traffic officer learnership pays a stipend of around R5 500 a month over the programme, plus a recognised traffic-officer qualification and a route into stable public-sector employment.

Application Process

Step by step
1
Watch provincial government and municipal sites for the advert
2
Complete the application form (often a Z83 for government posts)
3
Submit your matric, ID, driver's licence and CV
4
Pass physical, medical and integrity assessments
5
Begin the training programme if selected

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Qualified Traffic Officer
  • Provincial or Metro Traffic Officer
  • Road Safety Officer
  • Law-enforcement support roles
  • Progression within traffic and policing services

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the traffic officer learnership pay?
It pays a stipend of around R5 500 a month over the programme, plus a recognised traffic-officer qualification.
Do you need a driver's licence for the traffic officer learnership?
Yes. A valid driver's licence (often Code B) is typically required, along with physical fitness and a clear criminal record.
Who can apply for the traffic officer learnership?
South African citizens with a matric, a driver's licence, who are physically fit, with a clear record, aged roughly 18–35.
When does the traffic officer learnership close?
Intakes vary by province and often close mid-year. Confirm the exact date on the relevant provincial or municipal site.
