Source of Wealth

Adrian Gore's fortune of roughly $500 million in 2026 derives primarily from his stake of around 7% in Discovery Limited, the financial-services group he founded in 1992 and still leads as CEO.

Discovery's value rests on its Vitality shared-value model, which uses behavioural economics and data to reward customers for healthy living — an idea Gore has exported to the UK, US, and China.

The group posted a 24% rise in normalised operating profit to R8.9 billion in its half-year results to December 2025, and Discovery Bank crossed into profitability for the first time in the same period.

Companies & Major Holdings

  • Discovery Limited — Founder and CEO; ~7% stake in the JSE-listed group
  • Discovery Health — South Africa's largest medical scheme, covering ~2.8 million people
  • Vitality — The global shared-value wellness platform licensed across markets
  • Discovery Bank — The group's 'behavioural bank', now profitable

Lessons Learned

What aspiring builders can take away
  • Build around one big idea. The shared-value 'reward health' concept underpins everything Discovery does
  • Use data as an edge. Behavioural economics and data let Discovery price and prevent, not just pay claims
  • Export what works. Vitality became a licensed model across multiple countries
  • Set goals that cost you something. Gore treats ambitious goals as commitments, not just targets
