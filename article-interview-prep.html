How to Ace a Job Interview in South Africa

Most interviews are won or lost in preparation, not performance. Here's a practical framework that works for South African employers.

MzansiInsider6 min readUpdated June 2026

Research Like You Mean It

Before any interview, study the company, the role, and the people interviewing you. Know their products, recent news, and challenges. Walking in informed signals genuine interest and lets you tailor your answers.

Master the STAR Method

For competency questions ("Tell me about a time..."), answer with Situation, Task, Action, Result. It keeps you concise and shows impact. Prepare three or four strong STAR stories covering teamwork, problem-solving, and overcoming a challenge.

"The candidate who asks thoughtful questions at the end is the one who's remembered — interviews are a two-way conversation, not an interrogation."

Anticipate the Classics

Prepare for "Tell me about yourself", "Why this role?", "What are your weaknesses?", and "Where do you see yourself in five years?". Practise out loud until your answers feel natural, not rehearsed.

Have Questions Ready

Always ask questions. Good ones: "What does success look like in this role in the first six months?" or "What are the team's biggest priorities this year?" It shows engagement and helps you assess fit.

Follow Up

Send a short thank-you email within 24 hours, reaffirming your interest and one reason you're a strong fit. It's a small touch that many skip.

Don't Forget the Offer

When the offer comes, negotiate — see our salary negotiation guide. And make sure your CV got you there in the first place.

Frequently Asked Questions

How should I prepare for a job interview?
Research the company and role thoroughly, prepare STAR-method answers for competency questions, anticipate common questions, prepare your own questions, and plan a follow-up email.
What is the STAR method?
STAR stands for Situation, Task, Action, Result — a structure for answering competency questions concisely while clearly showing your impact.
What questions should I ask in an interview?
Ask thoughtful ones like 'What does success look like in the first six months?' or 'What are the team's biggest priorities this year?' to show engagement and assess fit.
Should I send a thank-you email after an interview?
Yes. A short thank-you email within 24 hours, reaffirming your interest and a key strength, is a small touch that helps you stand out.
