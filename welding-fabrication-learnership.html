About This Learnership

The Welding and Fabrication learnership, through the merSETA, trains learners in metal fabrication and welding techniques, leading to an NQF Level 3 qualification and a path toward an artisan trade test. Skilled trades offer strong, durable earnings.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 10–12 (Maths and Science an advantage)
  • Interest in welding, fabrication or engineering
  • Unemployed youth (roughly 18–35)
  • Physically able to do hands-on workshop work

Stipend & What You Get

The welding and fabrication learnership pays a stipend of around R5 000 a month, plus a merSETA qualification and a pathway toward an artisan trade test. Qualified welders and boilermakers can earn strong wages without a university degree.

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Welder or Metal Fabricator
  • Boilermaker (with trade test)
  • Workshop or Production Technician
  • Site Artisan in construction or mining
  • A self-employed fabrication contractor

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the welding learnership pay?
It pays a stipend of around R5 000 a month, plus a merSETA NQF 3 qualification and a path toward an artisan trade test.
Can you earn well as a welder without a degree?
Yes. Skilled trades like welding and boilermaking can pay strong wages without a university degree, especially after a trade test.
Do you need matric for a welding learnership?
Many welding learnerships accept Grade 10–12; Maths and Science are an advantage but not always essential.
Where do you find welding learnerships?
They are advertised by companies funded by the merSETA, on company careers pages and SETA portals.
