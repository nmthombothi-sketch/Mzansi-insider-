About This Bursary

The SARS Bursary funds students in accounting, law, economics, and IT — fields central to tax administration. It covers the cost of study and may carry a work-back commitment to serve at the revenue service after qualifying.

Requirements

  • South African citizen
  • Studying accounting, law, economics or IT
  • Strong academic results
  • Demonstrated financial need
  • Commitment to working at SARS after qualifying

What It Covers

  • Full tuition and registration
  • Accommodation and meals
  • Books and a living allowance
  • Practical exposure at SARS
  • A path to public-sector employment

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the SARS bursary fund?
Accounting, law, economics, and IT — fields central to tax administration and the revenue service.
Is there a work-back obligation?
A work-back commitment to serve at SARS after qualifying generally applies. Confirm the exact terms in your agreement.
Who qualifies for the SARS bursary?
South African citizens with strong academic results studying accounting, law, economics, or IT, with demonstrated financial need.
When does the SARS bursary close?
Applications usually close in the second half of the year. Confirm the exact date on the official SARS site.
