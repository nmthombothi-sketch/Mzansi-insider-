How to Apply for a Bursary in South Africa and Actually Win One

Bursaries are competitive, but a strong, early, well-documented application beats most. Here's how to maximise your chances.

MzansiInsider6 min readUpdated June 2026

Start Early and Apply Widely

The single biggest mistake is leaving it late. Bursary windows open and close on fixed dates, and the best programmes are heavily oversubscribed. Apply to several — see our bursary database — and track every deadline.

Match Yourself to the Right Bursaries

Each bursary has criteria: field of study, financial need, academic results, sometimes demographics. Apply to ones you genuinely fit — a targeted application beats a scattergun one.

"A complete, early application to ten well-matched bursaries beats a rushed application to thirty you barely qualify for."

Get Your Documents Ready

Most bursaries need a certified ID, academic records, proof of household income, and proof of acceptance or registration. Prepare these once, keep certified copies ready, and you'll apply far faster.

Write a Compelling Motivation

Where a motivation letter is required, be specific and genuine: why this field, why you, and what you'll do with the opportunity. Avoid generic templates — selectors read hundreds.

Honour the Conditions

Many bursaries carry work-back agreements. Understand them before accepting — see individual pages like Funza Lushaka or Sasol for the specifics.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I apply for a bursary in South Africa?
Start early, apply to several well-matched bursaries, prepare certified documents (ID, results, proof of income and registration), write a genuine motivation, and track every deadline.
What documents do I need for a bursary application?
Typically a certified ID, academic records, proof of household income, and proof of acceptance or registration. Prepare certified copies in advance.
What's the biggest mistake in bursary applications?
Leaving it late. Bursary windows close on fixed dates and the best programmes are heavily oversubscribed, so applying early to several is essential.
Do bursaries have conditions?
Many do — especially work-back agreements requiring you to work for the funder or in a sector after qualifying. Understand the conditions before accepting.
