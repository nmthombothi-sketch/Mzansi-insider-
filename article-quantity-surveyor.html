How to Become a Quantity Surveyor in South Africa

Quantity surveyors are the financial managers of construction. Here's how to qualify, register as a PrQS, and build a well-paid built-environment career.

MzansiInsider6 min readUpdated June 2026

What a QS Does

Quantity surveyors manage the cost and commercial side of construction projects — estimating, budgeting, and controlling costs from tender to completion. It pairs technical knowledge with commercial sharpness.

The Qualification

Study a BSc (Hons) in Quantity Surveying or Construction Economics at an accredited university. Strong Maths helps, as the work is numbers-heavy.

Professional Registration

After a candidate phase gaining experience, you register as a Professional Quantity Surveyor (PrQS) with the SACQSP. This is the key earnings driver and unlocks senior commercial roles.

"Registration as a PrQS is the milestone that separates a graduate salary from a senior commercial one — it's worth pursuing early."

Who Employs QSs

Major contractors like WBHO and Murray & Roberts, plus cost-consultancies and developers. See our QS salary guide for ranges.

Get Started

Apply for a BSc QS, and consider a construction learnership for early site exposure.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you become a quantity surveyor in South Africa?
Study a BSc (Hons) in Quantity Surveying or Construction Economics, complete a candidate phase gaining experience, then register as a Professional Quantity Surveyor (PrQS) with the SACQSP.
Does PrQS registration increase pay?
Yes. Registration as a Professional Quantity Surveyor with the SACQSP is a major pay driver and unlocks senior commercial and consulting roles.
Who employs quantity surveyors?
Major construction contractors such as WBHO and Murray & Roberts, along with cost-consultancies and property developers.
Is quantity surveying a good career?
Yes. It offers stable, well-paid work tied to construction and property, with earnings rising meaningfully after professional registration.
