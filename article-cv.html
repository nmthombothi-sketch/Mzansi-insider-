The South African CV That Actually Gets Interviews in 2026

Recruiters at top SA firms say 80% of CVs fail before the first page. Here is the exact two-page structure, the language that works, and the things that immediately disqualify you.

MzansiInsider7 min readUpdated June 2026

Keep It to Two Pages

South African recruiters spend an average of seven seconds on a first pass. A CV longer than two pages signals an inability to prioritise. Cut everything that isn't recent, relevant, or quantified.

The Structure That Works

  • Header: Name, city, phone, email, LinkedIn. No photo, no ID number, no marital status.
  • Professional summary: Three lines, tailored to the specific role.
  • Experience: Reverse chronological, each bullet starting with an action verb and ending with a measurable result.
  • Education & certifications.
  • Skills: Concrete and verifiable, not "good communicator".
"Show me numbers. 'Reduced reporting time by 40%' beats 'responsible for reporting' every single time."
— Talent Acquisition Manager, Johannesburg

What Disqualifies You Instantly

Spelling errors, a generic objective copied across applications, an unprofessional email address, and unexplained employment gaps. Address gaps briefly and move on — silence reads worse than honesty.

Beat the ATS

Most large SA employers screen CVs with applicant tracking software. Mirror the exact keywords from the job advert, use a clean single-column layout, and submit as a PDF unless told otherwise.
