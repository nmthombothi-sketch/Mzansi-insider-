About This Learnership

The Department of Labour's OHS learnership trains learners in occupational health and safety at NQF Level 4. OHS skills are needed in every sector — construction, manufacturing, mining, retail — making this a versatile qualification. Learners gain the knowledge to support workplace compliance, risk assessment, and safety management, with a stipend during the programme.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 / Matric
  • Aged 18–35 in most intakes
  • Interest in health, safety and compliance
  • Attention to detail and a responsible attitude

Stipend & What You Get

Learners receive a monthly stipend of around R4 000 over the 12-month programme while completing an accredited NQF 4 OHS qualification. The stipend supports basic living costs during the learnership.

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Health and safety officer / SHE representative roles
  • Compliance and risk-assessment positions
  • Safety roles across construction, manufacturing and mining
  • A foundation for further OHS qualifications (e.g. SAMTRAC)

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the OHS learnership pay?
It pays a monthly stipend of around R4 000 over the 12-month programme while you complete an accredited NQF 4 occupational health and safety qualification.
Is occupational health and safety a good career?
Yes. OHS skills are required in every industry — construction, manufacturing, mining, retail — which makes the qualification versatile and the roles relatively secure.
Who can apply for the OHS learnership?
South African citizens with a Grade 12 / Matric and an interest in health, safety, and compliance, with attention to detail.
What can the OHS learnership lead to?
Roles such as health and safety officer or SHE representative, and a foundation for further OHS qualifications like SAMTRAC.
