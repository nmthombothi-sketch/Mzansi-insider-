About This Learnership

The Contact Centre learnership, through the Services SETA, prepares learners for customer-service and call-centre roles, building communication, problem-solving, and client-relationship skills. South Africa's BPO sector serves global clients and hires at scale.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 / Matric
  • Clear spoken English and a good phone manner
  • Unemployed youth (roughly 18–35)
  • Computer literate and customer-focused

Stipend & What You Get

The contact centre learnership pays a stipend of around R5 000 a month over 12 months, plus an NQF 3 qualification. With South Africa a growing global outsourcing hub, these skills open steady call-centre and BPO work.

Career Opportunities After Completion

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the contact centre learnership pay?
It pays a stipend of around R5 000 a month over 12 months, plus an NQF 3 customer-service qualification.
Is the call-centre sector hiring in South Africa?
Yes. South Africa is a growing global outsourcing (BPO) hub, and the contact-centre sector hires at scale, including for international accounts.
What do you need for a contact centre learnership?
A matric certificate, clear spoken English, a good phone manner, and basic computer literacy.
Can a contact centre job lead anywhere?
Yes. Agents can progress to team leader, quality assurance, training, and international-account roles with experience.
