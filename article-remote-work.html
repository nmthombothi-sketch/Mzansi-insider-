How to Get Remote Work from South Africa (and Earn in Forex)

South Africans are well placed for global remote work — strong English, a useful time zone, and the rand advantage. Here's how to break in.

MzansiInsider7 min readUpdated June 2026

Why South Africans Have an Edge

South African remote workers are sought after globally for strong business English and a GMT+2 time zone that bridges European and American working hours. And because you earn in a stronger currency while spending in rands, the maths is compelling.

Build an In-Demand Skill

Remote work rewards portable, digital skills: software development, data, design, digital marketing, writing, and virtual assistance. See our most in-demand skills report to pick a direction.

"Earning dollars or euros while living on rands is one of the most powerful wealth-building advantages available to skilled South Africans today."

Where to Find Remote Roles

Global platforms (Upwork, Toptal, We Work Remotely, LinkedIn) list remote roles, and many international companies now hire directly through employer-of-record services. Build a strong profile and portfolio to compete.

Prove Your Skills

Remote employers can't watch you work, so they hire on demonstrated ability. A portfolio, GitHub, or track record matters more than credentials. Start with smaller contracts to build reviews and reputation.

Mind the Practicalities

Reliable internet, a professional setup, and disciplined communication are essential. And remember: forex income is still taxable in South Africa, so keep records and understand your obligations.

Consider a Side Hustle First

Many remote careers start as side hustles — freelance writing, virtual assistance, or tutoring — that grow into full remote income.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can South Africans work remotely for foreign companies?
Yes. South Africans are sought after for strong business English and a GMT+2 time zone, and increasingly hired through global platforms and employer-of-record services.
What skills are best for remote work?
Portable digital skills: software development, data, design, digital marketing, writing, and virtual assistance are all in demand for remote roles.
Why is remote work for foreign companies attractive?
You can earn in a stronger currency like dollars or euros while spending in rands, a powerful wealth-building advantage for skilled South Africans.
Is foreign remote income taxable in South Africa?
Yes. Income earned from foreign remote work is still taxable in South Africa, so it's important to keep records and understand your tax obligations.
