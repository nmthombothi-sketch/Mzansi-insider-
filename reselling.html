Reselling & Thrift Flipping in South Africa

One of the lowest-effort, lowest-risk hustles: start by selling things you already own, then flip underpriced second-hand finds for profit.

Overview

Reselling means buying underpriced items — thrifted clothing, vintage furniture, collectibles, or sale-rack bargains — and selling them at a markup. You can start with zero risk by first selling items you no longer use, learning the platforms and process before spending anything.

Marketplaces like Facebook Marketplace, Yaga, and Gumtree make listing easy, and the model scales as you learn what sells.

Realistic Income Breakdown

Realistic earnings run R1 000–R10 000 a month depending on the time you invest and how well you source. Begin by selling your own unused items for pure profit, then reinvest into underpriced stock — designer clothing, vintage pieces, and collectibles tend to flip well. Margins depend entirely on buying low.

How to Start

Step by step
1
Sell items you already own on Facebook Marketplace or Yaga to learn the ropes
2
Study what sells well and at what price in your area
3
Source underpriced stock from thrift stores, sales, and clearance
4
Photograph and list items clearly, with honest descriptions
5
Reinvest profits into more stock and build toward a niche

Pros

  • Near-zero startup risk
  • Start with what you own
  • Flexible and low-pressure
  • Scales with effort

Cons

  • Income depends on sourcing skill
  • Time spent hunting stock
  • Storage can become an issue
  • Some items don't sell quickly

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can you earn reselling in South Africa?
Realistic earnings run R1 000–R10 000 a month depending on time invested and sourcing skill. The key is buying underpriced stock and selling at a markup.
How do you start reselling with no money?
Start by selling items you already own on Facebook Marketplace or Yaga. This earns pure profit and teaches you the platforms before you spend anything on stock.
Where can you sell second-hand items in South Africa?
Facebook Marketplace, Yaga, and Gumtree are the most popular platforms, each making it easy to list and reach local buyers.
What items are best for reselling?
Designer clothing, vintage furniture, and collectibles tend to flip well, but the real skill is buying anything underpriced and selling it at a fair markup.

Don't Forget SARS

Side-hustle income is taxable. Keep records from day one, separate business from personal money, and set aside a portion for tax. Once you earn beyond the threshold you may need to register as a provisional taxpayer.
