About This Learnership

Capitec's banking learnership develops young people in retail banking, client service, and bank operations, leading to an NQF Level 4 banking qualification. As a fast-growing bank, Capitec frequently absorbs strong learners into permanent roles.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 / Matric with Mathematics or Maths Literacy
  • Aged roughly 18–30
  • Unemployed and not studying full-time
  • Strong communication and customer-service skills
  • Clear criminal and credit record (banking requirement)

Stipend & What You Get

Capitec's learnership pays a stipend of around R6 000 a month over 12 months, plus a nationally recognised NQF 4 banking qualification. As with other bank learnerships, the qualification and the path to permanent work are the main rewards.

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Bank Better Champion / Service Consultant
  • Teller or Client Service Consultant
  • Branch operations roles
  • Personal Banker (with experience)
  • Progression within Capitec's branch network

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the Capitec learnership pay?
The Capitec banking learnership pays a stipend of around R6 000 a month over 12 months, plus an NQF 4 banking qualification.
Do you need Maths for the Capitec learnership?
A matric pass is required, with Mathematics or Mathematical Literacy expected, plus strong communication skills.
Does the Capitec learnership require a clean record?
Yes. As a banking programme, it typically requires a clear criminal and credit record.
Does the Capitec learnership lead to a job?
Capitec frequently absorbs strong learners into permanent branch roles, though employment is not guaranteed.
