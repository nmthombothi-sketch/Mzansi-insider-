About This Learnership

Eskom's engineering learnership trains learners in electrical disciplines across power systems, distribution, and generation, combining structured theory with hands-on experience at Eskom facilities. It is among the most sought-after technical learnerships in South Africa.

Requirements

Who can apply
  • South African citizen
  • Grade 12 with Mathematics and Physical Science
  • Interest in electrical or power engineering
  • Unemployed youth (roughly 18–35)
  • Willingness to work at power stations and technical sites

Stipend & What You Get

Eskom offers one of the strongest stipends among SA learnerships, typically around R6 500 a month over the programme, plus an accredited NQF 4 engineering qualification and exposure to world-class training facilities.

Application Process

Step by step
1
Apply via the Eskom careers portal when the learnership opens
2
Select an electrical or power engineering stream
3
Upload matric (with Maths and Science), certified ID and CV
4
Complete assessments if shortlisted
5
Begin the programme if selected

Career Opportunities After Completion

  • Electrical Technician or Plant Operator
  • Engineering Technician at a power station
  • Artisan (with trade test)
  • Technical specialist roles with experience
  • Further study toward a National Diploma or BEng Tech

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the Eskom learnership pay?
Eskom offers one of the better stipends, typically around R6 500 a month, plus an accredited NQF 4 engineering qualification.
What subjects do you need for the Eskom learnership?
Grade 12 with Mathematics and Physical Science is required for the technical engineering streams.
When does the Eskom learnership close?
Some Eskom intakes close early in the year (around February). Confirm the exact date on the official Eskom careers portal.
Is the Eskom learnership competitive?
Yes, very. Eskom learnerships are highly sought after due to strong stipends and world-class training, so apply early with a complete application.
